# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## What is Vector Helper?
Vector Helper is a **conceptual AI accessibility assistant** designed to explore
how artificial intelligence could support users by reducing cognitive overload
and providing ethical, context-aware guidance.

It is a **vision and architectural concept**, not a finished product.

---

## Is Vector Helper a real device or application?
No.

Vector Helper is **not** a commercially available product, application, or
medical device. It exists as a research and design concept documented through
system architecture, ethics, and vision files.

---

## Why was this project created?
The project was created to:
- Explore accessibility-first AI design
- Address attention overload and digital fatigue
- Encourage ethical constraints in AI assistants
- Share ideas with the broader technology community

---

## Who is this project for?
Vector Helper is intended for:
- Researchers and students
- Accessibility advocates
- AI and UX designers
- Technology companies exploring assistive systems
- Anyone interested in ethical AI concepts

---

## Is this related to Google, Apple, Microsoft, or other companies?
No.

While the project references existing technologies and platforms for context,
it is an **independent initiative** and has no official affiliation, partnership,
or endorsement from any company.

---

## Can companies use this idea?
Yes — **as a concept**.

The author welcomes responsible, ethical use of the ideas presented, provided
proper attribution is given and the implementation respects accessibility and
ethical principles.

---

## Why is there no working prototype?
The project focuses on **conceptual design and system thinking**.

Building a real-world implementation would require:
- Significant technical resources
- Hardware development
- Regulatory approval
- Financial backing

This repository intentionally concentrates on the idea layer.

---

## Is this medical or assistive technology advice?
No.

Vector Helper does not provide medical, diagnostic, or therapeutic advice and is
not a certified assistive device.

---
